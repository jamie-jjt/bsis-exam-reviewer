1. Logical Reasoning & Aptitude

📖 Simple Explanation

Entrance exams for Information Systems evaluate your ability to recognize patterns and solve quantitative problems. Logic tests do not measure how many facts you know; they measure how you think. In programming and systems design, identifying patterns and rules is the core of problem-solving.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown

  • Number/Letter Series: A sequence of items following a hidden mathematical or alphabetical rule.
  • Analogies: Finding the relationship between two words (e.g., A is to B as C is to D).
  • Truth Tables: A fundamental IT concept showing how logical operators (AND, OR, NOT) affect outcomes.
Click to view Number Patterns & Series Examples

💡 Examples & Thinking Process

Numeric Pattern: 2, 4, 8, 16, ?

  1. Step 1: Find the difference between numbers. (4-2=2, 8-4=4, 16-8=8).
  2. Step 2: Recognize the pattern. The differences are doubling. Or, simply notice each number is multiplied by 2.
  3. Solution: 16 * 2 = 32.

Analogy: Keyboard : Type :: Monitor : ?

Thinking: A keyboard is an input device used to type. A monitor is an output device used to display. Answer: Display.

💡 Intro to Truth Tables

In Information Systems, computers only understand True (1) and False (0).

⚡ Quick Tips

  • Write it out: For number series, always write the difference between the numbers directly underneath them.
  • Read analogies backward: Sometimes the relationship is clearer if you reverse the order of the words.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Don't assume a series only uses one mathematical operation. A common trap is an alternating series (e.g., +2, -1, +2, -1).

2. Mathematics (College Entrance Level)

📖 Simple Explanation

Mathematics in entrance exams isn't about complex calculus; it's about basic algebra, fractions, and translating real-world problems into mathematical equations.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown

  • Algebra: Using letters (like x and y) to represent unknown values.
  • Fractions/Percentages: Representing parts of a whole. Remember: Percent literally means "per 100".
  • Statistics: Mean (Average), Median (Middle number), Mode (Most frequent number).
Click to view Algebra & Word Problem Examples

💡 Examples & Thinking Process

Equation: 3x + 6 = 18

  1. Goal: Isolate the variable (x).
  2. Step 1: Subtract 6 from both sides. (3x = 12)
  3. Step 2: Divide both sides by 3. (x = 4)

Word Problem (Structured Method):

Problem: A laptop originally costs ₱25,000. It is on sale for 20% off. What is the final price?

  • Given: Original Price = 25,000, Discount = 20% (or 0.20).
  • Required: Final Price.
  • Equation: Final Price = Original - (Original * Discount)
  • Solution: 25,000 - (25,000 * 0.20) = 25,000 - 5,000 = ₱20,000.

⚡ Quick Tips

Estimation is your friend. If a multiple-choice question asks for 18% of ₱1,025, calculate 20% of ₱1,000 in your head (₱200). Look for the answer choice slightly below ₱200.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Failing to follow PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction). For example, 2 + 3 * 4 is 14, not 20.

3. IT Fundamentals (CRITICAL)

📖 Simple Explanation

Information Systems (IS) go beyond technology. They involve People, Process, and Technology working together to manage information for organizational goals. Before writing code, you must understand how computer systems process data and how different tech disciplines overlap.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: CS vs IT vs IS

Computer Science (CS) Info Technology (IT) Info Systems (BSIS)
Focuses on the theory of computing, algorithms, and writing core software. (The "Inventors") Focuses on hardware, networks, and infrastructure. Keeping systems running. (The "Builders/Mechanics") Focuses on how technology solves business problems. Bridge between business & tech. (The "Architects")

💡 Software & Databases

  • System Software: Includes the OS (Windows, Linux, macOS) and utility programs.
  • Application Software: Programs for users (Word processors, Browsers, Games).

Databases: Think of a database like a digital filing cabinet. While Excel is good for small lists, Databases are better for huge amounts of data because they are faster, safer, and can link different types of information together.

  • Table: The whole spreadsheet (e.g., "Student Roster").
  • Row (Record): One specific student.
  • Column (Field): One specific detail (e.g., "Phone Number").

Cloud Computing: Storing and accessing data/programs over the internet instead of your computer's hard drive (e.g., AWS, Google Drive).

🇵🇭 Did You Know?

The Philippine National ID System (PhilSys) is a massive real-world Information System. It collects Input (biometrics), Processes/Stores it in a secure Database, and generates an Output (the physical ID and digital verification).

4. Web & Technology Basics

📖 Simple Explanation

Modern Information Systems are almost always web-based. The internet is a massive network of cables and routers, while the World Wide Web is the system of linked documents accessed via the internet.

⚙️ Client-Server Architecture

CLIENT
(Your Phone/Browser)
⇄
SERVER
(Remote Computer storing the website)

The Client requests data (e.g., typing facebook.com). The Server processes the request and sends back the webpage files.

💡 The Holy Trinity of Web Development

  • HTML (Structure): The skeleton of a webpage. Tells the browser where headings, paragraphs, and images go.
  • CSS (Style): The clothes/makeup. Controls colors, fonts, and layouts.
  • JavaScript (Behavior): The muscle. Makes the website interactive (like popups or submitting forms).

5. Networking Basics

📖 Simple Explanation

Networking is the backbone of Information Systems. It allows devices to communicate and share resources. Understanding networks is crucial for designing systems that connect people, data, and applications.

💡 Layer Functions

  • Physical (1): Cables, switches, electrical signals.
  • Data Link (2): MAC addresses, error detection (e.g., Ethernet).
  • Network (3): IP addresses, routing (e.g., IP, routers).
  • Transport (4): Reliable delivery (e.g., TCP, UDP).
  • Session (5): Manages connections between applications.
  • Presentation (6): Data formatting, encryption.
  • Application (7): User-facing protocols (e.g., HTTP, FTP).

⚡ Quick Tips

  • Mnemonic: "Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away" for layers 1-7.
  • Real-world: When you browse a website, data travels from Application (browser) down to Physical (network cable) and back up.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Confusing layers: Remember, lower layers handle hardware/technical details, higher layers handle user/application logic.

6. Programming Fundamentals

📖 Simple Explanation

Programming is the process of writing instructions for computers to solve problems. In BSIS, you'll learn to automate processes, analyze data, and build applications that support business needs.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: Key Concepts

  • Algorithms: Step-by-step procedures to solve problems.
  • Variables: Containers for storing data (e.g., numbers, text).
  • Control Structures: Loops (repeat actions) and conditionals (if-then decisions).
  • Functions: Reusable blocks of code.

7. Database Management

📖 Simple Explanation

Databases store and organize data for easy retrieval. In IS, databases power everything from student records to e-commerce transactions.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: Database Concepts

  • Tables: Collections of related data (e.g., Students table).
  • Records/Rows: Individual entries in a table.
  • Fields/Columns: Categories of data (e.g., Name, Age).
  • Keys: Primary (unique identifier) and Foreign (links tables).
Click to view ER Diagram Example

💡 Entity-Relationship Diagram

ER diagrams show relationships between entities. Example: Students enroll in Courses.

ID, Name, Email

Code, Title, Credits

Relationship: One student can enroll in many courses (many-to-many).

💡 Basic SQL Commands

  • SELECT: Retrieve data (e.g., SELECT name FROM students).
  • INSERT: Add new records.
  • UPDATE: Modify existing data.
  • DELETE: Remove records.

⚡ Quick Tips

  • Normalization: Organize data to reduce redundancy (e.g., separate addresses into their own table).
  • Backup: Always backup databases to prevent data loss.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

SQL injection: Never insert user input directly into queries; use prepared statements.

9. Systems Analysis & Design

📖 Simple Explanation

Systems Analysis & Design (SAD) is the process of planning and creating information systems. It involves understanding user needs and designing solutions.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: SDLC Phases

  1. Planning: Define scope and objectives.
  2. Analysis: Gather requirements from users.
  3. Design: Create system specifications.
  4. Implementation: Build the system.
  5. Testing: Verify functionality.
  6. Deployment: Roll out to users.
  7. Maintenance: Ongoing support and updates.

10. Data & Information Concepts

📖 Simple Explanation

In everyday language, "data" and "information" are used interchangeably. In Information Systems, they are completely different. Your job in BSIS is to transform data INTO information.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: Data vs Information

Data Information
Raw, unorganized facts and figures. Processed, organized, and meaningful data.
Example: "15", "Mango", "50" Example: "We sold 15 Mangoes today for ₱50 each."

💡 The Data Lifecycle (Real-world Business)

  1. Collection: A Jollibee cashier inputs your order into the POS system (TPS).
  2. Storage: The order data is saved in the company's central database.
  3. Processing: The system calculates daily totals.
  4. Analysis: Managers review the Information (e.g., "Chickenjoy sales drop on rainy days") to make business decisions.

⚡ Quick Tips

If an exam asks to identify "Data," look for isolated numbers or words with no context. If it asks for "Information," look for sentences, reports, or summarized graphs.

11. Reading Comprehension

📖 Simple Explanation

College entrance exams feature long reading passages to test how well you absorb, analyze, and retain written information under time pressure.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: Techniques

  • Skimming: Reading rapidly to get the main idea or general overview. (Looking at headings, first sentences).
  • Scanning: Looking rapidly for a specific piece of information (like a date, name, or statistic).
  • Context Clues: Guessing the meaning of a difficult word based on the words surrounding it.

⚡ Quick Tips

Always read the questions FIRST. Before reading a massive 5-paragraph essay, read the questions. This primes your brain to "scan" for the exact answers you need, saving valuable time.

12. English & Communication

📖 Simple Explanation

IT professionals must write clear emails, documentation, and system requirements. Grammar exams test your understanding of structural rules in English.

⚙️ Structured Breakdown: Key Rules

Subject-Verb Agreement: Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs.

  • Singular: The server (subject) is (verb) offline.
  • Plural: The servers (subject) are (verb) offline.

⚠️ Common Exam Traps

Trap 1: Prepositional Phrases. Exams will put extra words between the subject and the verb to confuse you.

Incorrect: The list of student names are on the desk.

Correct: The list (of student names) is on the desk. (The subject is 'list', not 'names').
